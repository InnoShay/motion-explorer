💡 Key Concept
Distance is the total path length. Displacement is the shortest straight-line distance from start to end, with direction.
🏆 Mini Challenge
Which value always represents the shortest route between start and end?
⚠️ Common Confusion

Distance ≠ Displacement! Distance is always positive and depends on the path taken. Displacement can be zero (if you return to the start) and depends only on the starting and ending positions.

💡 Key Concept
Speed = How fast (magnitude only). Velocity = How fast + in which direction (magnitude + direction).
🗺️
Real-Life Connection: Google Maps Google Maps doesn't just show speed — it shows direction too. That's velocity in action!
🏆 Mini Challenge
Can two objects have the same speed but different velocities?
⚠️ Common Confusion

Speed ≠ Velocity! Speed has no direction. Two cars moving at 60 km/h — one North, one South — have the same speed but different velocities.

💡 Key Concept
Acceleration = Change in Velocity ÷ Time Taken. It tells us how quickly the velocity is changing.
🟢 Positive Acceleration

Speed is increasing.
Example: Car speeding up

🔴 Negative Acceleration

Speed is decreasing.
Example: Car braking

🏆 Mini Challenge
What happens when speed increases by the same amount every second?
⚠️ Common Confusion

Acceleration does NOT always mean moving fast! A bus moving at constant high speed has zero acceleration. Acceleration is about the CHANGE in velocity, not the speed itself.
